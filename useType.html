What is the main use of your PC

Before diving into part selection we must first ask ourselves, what is the main purpose of this PC? Will this new PC be used in the living room as an all-in-one streaming device on your TV? Or maybe it will be used for video editing and rendering? Whatever the case may be, there are a few things to consider from the start

Budget How much do you actually want to spend on this new system. Yes most of the time we can save money when building our own system but it is also very easy to purchase parts that may be overkill and blow through the budget.

Form Factor Computer cases come in all shapes and sizes. Obviously there are pros and cons to having a very tiny case or a very large one. To make things simple we can thing of three main sizes: mini tower, mid tower and full tower (mid tower is the most common size).

Actual purpose of the PC Computers can do just about anything now days but they must be built for the task. To simplify things I have broken this down into three main categories

Basic User

This category is aimed at users who simply want a good all-around computer without breaking the bank. A PC for a basic user needs to brows the web, check emails, stream videos, and maybe some light gaming (not suitable for 3D AAA games).

The Gamer

The name of this category speaks for itself, a gaming computer. A gaming PC can range widely in price based on the users desire, its all about the FPS. A PC for this user should easily do everything a basic computer can do as well run the latest games at minimum 1080 resolution.

The Professional

This category is for the more specific user that will generally use this PC for graphically / computationally demanding work. Special consideration may be required when choosing the processor and graphics card based on the type of work.

Ok so hopefully you have a better idea of what kind of user you are and what you will require of your PC. When your ready lets dive in to the components!
