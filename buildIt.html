Build It

Step 1

Prep the Motherboard

The first thing I do with a new build is “prep” the motherboard, by this I mean install the CPU and its cooler before putting it in the case. To install the CPU simply unlatch the socket holder and remove the plastic protecting guard. Remove the CPU from its package, to handle it simply hold it by the sides, avoid touching the underside with all the pins. Looking at the chip you will see it has notches on some of the sides, this is to make installation easy, line up the notches with the ones on the motherboard and drop it in. You will not have to put pressure, if it doesn’t go in properly take it out and try again. Once the CPU is in its slot on the motherboard close and secure the latch.

Step 2

Install the memory stick(s)

Most standard motherboards come with 2, 4, or 8 slots to install memory (RAM). Depending on the memory configuration you chose (2x4GB sticks, 1x8GB stick, 4x8GB sicks, etc.) your installation may very. When not filling up all the slots (2 ram sticks on a 4 slot motherboard for example) the slots you use will affect RAM speed. The best thing to do is look into the manual for the motherboard to see the optimal configuration. Usually this configuration skips a slot so slot 1 and slot 3 for example. To install simply match the notch in the connection with the ones on your ram slots then push until they click in.

Step 3

Install the CPU Cooler Bracket

In many cases the CPU cooler needs to use the included bracket to mount on the motherboard. Many of these brackets need to access behind the motherboard, this is the reason I like to do this before mounting the motherboard in the case. Some cases offer a cut-out to access this part of the motherboard once mounted but not all. As there are many different types of coolers and brackets I will not cover exactly how to mount this, simply follow the provided instructions and you should be fine.

Step 4

Apply thermal paste and install the cooler

Now that the cooler bracket is installed you are ready to apply the thermal paste. Most thermal paste will come in a syringe like tube, simply apply a pea size amount on the middle of the top part of the CPU, NOT on the pins.

Step 5

Install the motherboard inside the case

It is now almost time to mount the motherboard in the case. Before mounting it you should look at your case fans positions and orientations. A common way to do this is to have all the fans in the front of the case used as air intake and all the fans towards the back/top as exhaust. Doing this allows for cool air to enter at the front and exhaust the hot air out the back. Also if you are using a water cooling block now is the time to decide where you will / can mount the radiator, intake or exhaust generally doesn’t make a huge difference for the radiator. Once all your fans are in place its time to mount the motherboard. ! Install the IO shield before mounting the motherboard. Lay the case flat on its back and line up the motherboard mounting holes with the ones inside the case. Use your magnetic screwdriver and slowly screw in the mounting screws, don’t overtighten them.

Step 6

Install the power supply inside the case

Look on the back of your case for the big square cut-out to find the location of your power supply, most new cases have it on the bottom but some on top. Route the power supply’s cables inside the case from the cut-out followed by the power supply itself, the switch should face the outside of the case. Add the four mounting screws and your done.

Step 7

Mount the hard drives in their bay

All cases will over hard drive bays, most of them will also offer a removable tray. Match the mounting holes on the trays with the ones on your hard drive / SSD and secure them. Once on the trays slide / screw in the trays back in their position. ! Be mindful of where the connections are on the drive so that they are still accessible once mounted. All drives need a SATA connection and power from the power supply, the connections have unique size and a notch to prevent improper installation. Match these notches and plug in the power and SATA cable.

Step 8

Plug in remaining components

Now it is time to start routing all the cables to their final destinations. You should have fan connectors, SATA connector(s), case connectors, and power connectors at the minimum. Look at your motherboards manual for the locations of all the connections (The case connectors are the cables that come attached to your case to control the power button, usb, headphone jack, etc.). For the case fans, route the cables to their closest input pin on the motherboard and plug them in, use a splitter if you have more fans than connections. Follow the labels on the case connectors and match them to the motherboard manual, same with SATA. Now all you need to add is power, there should be a CPU 8 pin connector towards the top left corner and a 24 pin connector on the right side, connect these from your power supply (pin size and location may vary, check manual).

Step 9

Cable management

Some people might say this is optional. The idea in this step is simply to tuck away all remaining cables, tie them down to the case and simply make your new system look good.

Step 10

Install GPU / other misc cards

Almost done!! It is now time to install the graphics card and any other miscellaneous cards (like a WiFi card for example). If you don’t have any of these parts skip this step. The graphic card will almost always go in the first (top) PCIE slot. ! Before trying to insert the card into the slot make sure the back cover is removed from the case. Simply slid in the card into the slot until it clicks, that’s it!

Step 11

Clean up and post

Sit the computer right side up and slide / screw on the side panels. The back panel may be a little harder to put on as this is usually where many extra cables get tucked away. Now plug in the power, screen, keyboard, and mouse. Cross your fingers and push the power button. Now you will probably have a mini panick attack as nothing happened, take a deep breath and check that the switch on the power supply is on. Repeat and exhale when it actually comes alive.

Step 12

Pat yourself on the back

You did it, you built your first PC! Install your desired operating system and enjoy!
